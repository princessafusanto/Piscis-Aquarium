Here are the marine animals list!

Adult bottlenose dolphins can reach 6 to 12 feet in length and weigh 400 to 800 pounds.
Atlantic Bottlenose Dolphin
The blue blubber jelly actually ranges in color from white to light blue to dark purple.
Blue Blubber Jellyfish
Cuttlefish have not one, not two, but three hearts!
Cuttle Fish
Discus fish are not just monogamous lovers, but they are also caring and responsible parents.
Discus
This fish can have a maximum lifespan of 35 years which is much longer than the honey gourami.
Eastern Blue Groper
They are tube dwellers, in tough, mucous tubes, coated with sand, mud and debris.
Fan Worm
The green sea turtle gets its name not from the color of its shell but from the greenish shade of its fat.
Green Sea Turtle
Humpback whales can live for 80 to 90 years.
Humpback Whale
These turtles eat snails and plant matter; females may be completely herbivorous.
Irwin's Snapping Turtle
The largetooth sawfish is circumtropical in coastal waters and in some river systems, including the famed Lake Nicaragua population.
Largetooth Sawfish
The bell of a moon jelly can be up to 12 inches wide, about the size of a dinner plate.
Moon JellyFish
New Zealand fur seals can be distinguished from sea lions by their pointy nose and smaller size.
New Zealand Fur Seal
Orca can be seen in every ocean on the earth. They are one of the most successful marine mammals in terms of range
Orca
Polar bears are classified as marine mammals.
Polar Bear
They are characterized by elongated, tapering bodies and a continuous long dorsal fin.
Queensland Blenny
The roughtail stingray is capable of inflicting a painful wound with the spines that line its tail.
Roughtail Stingray
Sand tiger sharks may look menacing with their pointed teeth but are considered sluggish and not a threat to humans.
Sand Tiger Shark
They use conotoxin to kill their prey, and it is strong enough to kill 60 adult humans!
Textile Cone Snail
Whale sharks are not whales, they are SHARKS!
Whale Shark
They are highly migratory and are found throughout the Pacific, Atlantic and Indian Oceans.
Yellow Fin Tuna
They are at the base of the food chain, feeding on microscopic plants and being fed upon by aquatic insects, fish and salamanders.
Zoo Plankton
